Understanding the conditions

Why these foods matter

Two conditions shape this guide. A Schatzki ring is a narrow band of tissue at the lower esophagus that makes swallowing difficult — especially with dry, chewy, or dense foods. Eosinophilic esophagitis (EoE) is an immune-mediated condition where certain foods trigger inflammation and swelling in the esophagus, sometimes forming rings over time.

Together, they require a diet that is both soft in texture (to prevent food getting stuck) and free of common allergens (to prevent immune flares). Every item on this list has been selected with both in mind.

Always avoid these triggers

These are the six most common EoE triggers. Check every label carefully — they hide under many names.

Dairy / milk Wheat / gluten Eggs Soy Tree nuts & peanuts Shellfish
General eating habits that help

Eat slowly, chew thoroughly, and take small bites — especially with proteins. Stay upright for at least 30 minutes after meals. Keep portions small to avoid pressure on the lower esophagus. Sip still water between bites to help move food down.

At the store

Reading labels carefully

Hidden triggers are the biggest challenge. These categories disguise themselves under ingredient names most people don't recognise.

Soy hides as...

Soy lecithin, edamame, miso, tamari, tempeh, textured vegetable protein (TVP), hydrolyzed soy protein.

Gluten hides as...

Malt, barley, wheat starch, spelt, kamut, triticale, durum, semolina, farro — and "natural flavors" can sometimes contain gluten.

Dairy hides as...

Casein, whey, lactose, lactalbumin, ghee, butter solids — even in products that don't taste like dairy.

Eggs hide as...

Albumin, globulin, lysozyme, mayonnaise, meringue, ovalbumin — and "lecithin" can be egg-derived (not always soy).

For proteins

Buy in bulk and freeze portions. Salmon, tilapia, and ground turkey freeze beautifully and make weeknight cooking effortless.

For dairy-free products

Choose coconut yogurt and oat milks with the shortest ingredient lists possible. Fewer ingredients means fewer chances for a hidden trigger.

Be consistent

When you find a brand that works, stick with it. EoE symptoms can take days to appear after a trigger, making it hard to pinpoint new products.

Avoid carbonation

Fizzy drinks create pressure in the esophagus. Stick to still water and gentle herbal teas — ginger tea is especially soothing.

Important

A note for family members

This guide is a helpful starting point — not a substitute for medical advice. Both conditions are managed differently depending on their severity, and a gastroenterologist may recommend specific treatments such as a structured elimination diet with systematic food reintroduction (common for EoE), or an esophageal dilation procedure (common for a Schatzki ring).

If you are preparing meals for someone with these conditions, the safest approach is to ask them directly about their current trigger list, since it varies from person to person. A registered dietitian familiar with EoE and esophageal conditions is an excellent resource for personalizing this guide further.

When to seek urgent care

If food becomes lodged in the throat or esophagus and does not pass on its own, this is a medical emergency. Do not attempt to dislodge it with more food or water — seek emergency care immediately.
